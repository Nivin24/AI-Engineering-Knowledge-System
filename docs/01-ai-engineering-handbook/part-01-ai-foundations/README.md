# Part 01 — AI Foundations

> **Understanding the discipline before learning the technologies.**

Artificial Intelligence is more than machine learning models and large language models. Modern AI systems are the result of decades of research combined with advances in software engineering, data engineering, distributed systems, cloud computing, and human-computer interaction.

This part establishes the conceptual foundation required for the remainder of the AI Engineering Handbook. Every subsequent topic—from Machine Learning and LLM Engineering to MLOps and Enterprise AI—builds upon the principles introduced here.

Rather than teaching specific tools or frameworks, this part focuses on developing the mental models needed to understand how modern AI systems are designed, built, deployed, and maintained.

## Purpose

The objective of this part is to answer the fundamental questions that every AI engineer should understand before studying implementation details.

These questions include:

- What is Artificial Intelligence?
- What is AI Engineering?
- Why has AI Engineering emerged as a distinct discipline?
- How has Artificial Intelligence evolved over time?
- How do modern AI systems differ from traditional software systems?
- What skills are expected from an AI Engineer?
- How do organizations design, deploy, and operate AI-powered products?

Understanding these concepts first provides the context needed to make informed engineering decisions throughout the rest of the handbook.

---

## Who Should Read This Part?

This part is intended for:

- Students beginning their AI Engineering journey.
- Software Engineers transitioning into AI.
- Data Scientists expanding into production AI systems.
- Machine Learning Engineers seeking broader engineering knowledge.
- AI professionals who want a structured understanding of the modern AI ecosystem.
- Technical interview candidates preparing for AI engineering roles.

After completing this part, you should be able to:

- Explain the evolution of Artificial Intelligence.

---

## Why This Part Matters

Many learning resources begin immediately with programming languages, machine learning algorithms, or deep learning frameworks.

This handbook takes a different approach.

Before learning **how** to build AI systems, it is essential to understand:

- Why AI systems exist.
- What business problems they solve.
- How AI integrates with software engineering.
- How AI systems are deployed and maintained in production.
- How the AI industry has evolved.
- Where AI engineering is heading.

A strong conceptual foundation makes every technical topic easier to understand and apply.
