# Scenario-Based Questions

These questions evaluate practical engineering judgment.

### Scenario 1

A company wants to automate customer support.

Would you recommend:

- Rule-based systems?
- Traditional Machine Learning?
- Foundation Models?
- AI Agents?

Explain your reasoning and discuss the trade-offs.

---

### Scenario 2

# Whiteboard Questions

Practice explaining these concepts without notes.

- Draw the historical evolution of AI.
- Compare Symbolic AI and Machine Learning.
- Explain the AI engineering lifecycle.
- Illustrate the transition from traditional software to AI Agents.
- Describe how the role of the AI engineer has evolved.

---

# Discussion Questions

These open-ended questions encourage deeper reflection.

- Will AI continue evolving beyond autonomous agents?
- Could another AI Winter occur? Why or why not?
- Should all business problems be solved using Generative AI?
- What responsibilities do AI engineers have when deploying intelligent systems?
- Which historical AI breakthrough do you believe had the greatest long-term impact, and why?

There are no single correct answers. Strong responses should demonstrate critical thinking, balanced reasoning, and awareness of engineering trade-offs.

# Preparing for Technical Interviews

The concepts covered in this chapter frequently appear in interviews for roles such as:

- AI Engineer
- Machine Learning Engineer
- LLM Engineer
- MLOps Engineer
- Data Scientist
- Software Engineer (AI-focused)
- AI Solutions Architect

While interviews often emphasize technical implementation, strong candidates are also expected to understand the historical context, architectural evolution, and engineering trade-offs that shaped modern AI systems.

A clear understanding of these concepts will strengthen your ability to discuss system design, technology selection, and long-term engineering decisions.
